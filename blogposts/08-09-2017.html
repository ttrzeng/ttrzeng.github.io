Gonna science the shit out of myself

August 9 2017

Recently, I have started tracking my own finances and spendings. After a few months of collecting the data, I have found something really interesting that I have never noticed. For the month of June, I have spent over $500 eating out at restaurants and $50 on groceries. Comparing to the month of July, I have only spent $150 on grocery and $50 eating out. That's a $300 difference! If I haven't been tracking my spendings, I would have never noticed how much money I have been spending every month; if I had a conscious idea of what I have done, I would have been more responsible with my spendings. Although it seems that tracking our daily spendings is a trivial task but not many actually try and understand where money comes from and where it goes (unless you are an accountant).

After noticing these unconscious spendings, I started to question what other bad habits that I might have missed by not collecting data and analyzing on them. Come to think of it, improving oneself can be broken down like a science experiment. We would first need a controlled environment, data, theory and most importantly analysis (it's like going back to school all over again).

First, there are a few categories that I believe is important to analyze in order to become a stronger version of myself; these categories can be broken down to subcategories for more specific analysis.

  • 1. Health: Calorie and nutrition
  • 2. Time management
  • 3. Sleep quality
  • 4. Energy management
